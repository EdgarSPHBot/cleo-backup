David shared Hannah's data-driven pacing protocol, derived from 774 days of WHOOP + Visible data. Key finding: **3-day lag** — how you feel today is set by what you did 3 days ago.

#### The Repair Spectrum (full spec)

**🔴 Red Day (recovery < 34)**
- Full rest. No negotiation. Cognitive rest too — limit screens, intense reading, stressful conversations.
- Any spend now extends the crash by days.

**🟡 Yellow Day (recovery 34–66)**
- Gentle only. Light walking < 20 min, PacePoints ≤ 8.
- Yellow feels like permission. It's not. Reserves aren't rebuilt yet.

**🟢 First Green Day after a bad stretch**
- **Rest day. Even though you feel good.** Hardest rule, most important one.
- PacePoints ≤ 8, one enjoyable low-effort thing.
- Spending it → crash in ~10 days. Resting through it → ~29 days of stability.

**🟢🟢 Second Consecutive Green Day**
- Light activity OK. One thing. PacePoints ≤ 12. Rest after, don't stack.
- This is when repair actually begins.

**🟢🟢🟢 Third Green Day and Beyond**
- Functional day earned. PacePoints ≤ 14.
- Avoid strain spikes ≥ 6 — one high-effort day resets the clock.
- Dec 14–19 reference run: 4.2–4.4 strain throughout. Low and steady held it.

**⚠️ Lag-3 Watch**
- If PacePoints were > 14 three days ago → treat today as yellow regardless of WHOOP.
- Strongest predictor in the data (−0.74). App will flag this.

#### Sleep Anchor (applies before everything else)
- Sleep performance < 60% → treat as yellow
- Sleep performance < 40% → treat as red
- Two bad sleep nights in a row → mandatory rest day

#### The Three Levers (in order)
1. **Sleep** — protect above everything else
2. **Pacing on good days** — especially that first green day
3. **Gut health** — SIM01, consistently, over months

All three must work together long enough for the loop to downshift.
